1Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
2Sec. 1.  24-A MRSA §2169-C is enacted to read:
3§2169-C.  Use of credit information prohibited in certain insurance practices
4 Notwithstanding any law to the contrary, with respect to insurance policies covering 
5 private passenger motor vehicles or commercial vehicles, an insurer may not use the credit 
6 information of an applicant or policyholder as a factor, in whole or in part, in making any 
7 of the following determinations related to the applicant or policyholder:
81.  Refusal to underwrite.  Refusing to underwrite a policy; 
92.  Cancellation.  Cancelling a policy; 
103.  Refusal to renew.  Refusing to renew a policy; 
114.  Premiums.  Increasing the policyholder’s premiums; 
125.  Discounts.  Applying or removing a discount; 
136.  Risk rating tiers.  Assigning the applicant or policyholder to a particular risk rating 
14 tier; 
157.  Billing or payment plans.  Assigning the applicant or policyholder to a particular 
16 billing or payment plan; or  
178.  Affiliated company.  Placing the applicant or policyholder with an affiliated 
18 company. "Affiliated company" has the same meaning as in section 6701, subsection 1. 
19 As used in this section, "commercial vehicle" has the same meaning as in Title 17, 
20 section 2263, subsection 4-A, "credit information" has the same meaning as in section 
21 2169-B, subsection 1, paragraph F and "private passenger motor vehicle" has the same 
22 meaning as in section 2927, subsection 1, paragraph D.
23SUMMARY
24 This bill prohibits insurers from using the credit information of an applicant or 
25 policyholder as a factor in making any of several determinations regarding policies 
26 covering private passenger motor vehicles and commercial vehicles. The bill provides that 
27 the prohibition applies notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary.
