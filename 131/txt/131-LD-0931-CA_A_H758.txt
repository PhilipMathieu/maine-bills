20 SUMMARY
21 This amendment, which is the maj ority report of the committee, replaces the bill, which
22 is a concept draft, with a resolve. It requires the Department of Corrections to conduct a
23 review of its reentry planning and support services to identify gaps that may exist between
24 the services that are provided and the needs of residents of correctional facilities who are
25 transitioning from incarceration to employment. It also requires the department to collect
26 statistical data on the cause of death for persons who die while on probation, The
27 department must work with key stakeholders, including, but not limited to: community-
28 based organizations that provide services to and advocate for persons who are or have been
29 incarcerated; employers and organizations representing employers that are interested in
30 hiring persons who were previously incarcerated; a person with lived experience as an
31 incarcerated person; and a representative of a victim advocacy organization, The
32 department must submit a report, by January 31, 2025, to the joint standing committee of
33 the Legislature having jurisdiction over criminal justice matters of the department's
34 findings, an evaluation of any gaps that may exist between the services the department
35 provides and the needs of persons transitioning from incarceration to employment, and the
36 department's recommendations for how best to meet to those needs, and statistical data on
37 the cause of death for persons who die while on probation. The committee may submit
3 8 legislation related to the report to the 1 32nd Legislature in 2025.
