13 SUMMARY
14 This amendment replaces the bill. The amendment requires a person who purchases a
15 lifetime license to hunt, trap or fish after December 31,2024 and who is 16 years of age or
16 older to notify the Department of Inland Fisheries and Wildlife in each year the license
17 holder intends to use the license by completing a renewal process with the department in
18 the same year that person intends to use the license. The amendment directs the
19 Commissioner of Inland Fisheries and Wildlife to encourage a lifetime license holder who
20 purchased a lifetime license to hunt, trap or fish on or prior to December 31, 2024 to
21 participate in the renewal process. It specifies that the department may not charge a fee for
22 the annual renewal of a lifetime license.
23 The amendment directs the department to submit, by February 1, 2027, a report to the
24 joint standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over inland fisheries and
25 wildlife matters that describes the online notification process and any additional outreach
26 initiatives used by the department to gauge user participation and obtain up-to-date user
27 information. The department must include in its report information on the efficacy of
28 enacting a reporting requirement for lifetime license holders that does not carry a penalty
29 and recommended changes, if any. The committee is authorized to report out a bill based
30 on the report to the 133rd Legislature in 2027.
