1Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
2Sec. 1.  22 MRSA §9067 is enacted to read:
3§9067.  Background checks for emergency medical services persons and emergency 
4medical services ambulance operators
51. Definitions. As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 
6 following terms have the following meanings.
7 A. "Emergency medical services ambulance operator" has the same meaning as in Title 
8 32, section 83, subsection 12-A.
9 B. "Emergency medical services person" has the same meaning as in Title 32, section 
10 83, subsection 12.
11 C. "Maine Emergency Medical Services" has the same meaning as in Title 32, section 
12 83, subsection 16-A.
132. Authorization. For the purposes of this chapter, Maine Emergency Medical 
14 Services may function as an employer to seek background check information through the 
15 Background Check Center on an applicant for licensure or relicensure under Title 32, 
16 chapter 2-B as an emergency medical services ambulance operator or an emergency 
17 medical services person.
183.  Background check report content. Maine Emergency Medical Services is entitled 
19 to all information obtained through the background check and rap back monitoring 
20 program, including offenses that would not be considered disqualifying or potentially 
21 disqualifying offenses under this chapter, for an applicant for licensure or relicensure under 
22 Title 32, chapter 2-B as an emergency medical services ambulance operator or an 
23 emergency medical services person. 
244.  Fees. Maine Emergency Medical Services may pass any fees or charges associated 
25 with participation in the Background Check Center on to an applicant for licensure or 
26 relicensure under Title 32, chapter 2-B as an emergency medical services ambulance 
27 operator or an emergency medical services person.
285.  Waiver of penalties. Maine Emergency Medical Services is not subject to the 
29 penalties specified in section 9062. 
30Sec. 2.  32 MRSA §85, sub-§3, as amended by PL 2013, c. 267, Pt. B, §25, is further 
31 amended by amending the first blocked paragraph to read:
32 The board shall obtain criminal history record information containing a record of public 
33 criminal history record information as defined in Title 16, section 703, subsection 9  8 for 
34 an applicant seeking licensure under this subsection.  Information obtained pursuant to this 
35 subsection is confidential and may be used only to determine suitability for issuance of a 
36 license to provide emergency medical services.  The results of criminal history record 
37 information checks received by the board are for official use only and may not be 
38 disseminated outside the board.  The applicant for licensure shall pay the expense of 
39 obtaining the information required by this subsection. The board may use the Background 
40 Check Center as defined in Title 22, section 9053, subsection 4 to conduct background 
41 checks for initial licensure of an emergency medical services person.

152.  Background check.  The board shall obtain criminal history record information 
16 containing a record of public criminal history record information as defined in Title 16, 
17 section 703, subsection 8 for an applicant for licensure under this section.  Information 
18 obtained pursuant to this subsection is confidential and may be used only to determine

24 to conduct background checks for initial licensure or relicensure of an emergency medical 
25 services ambulance operator.
26SUMMARY
27 This bill authorizes the use of the Background Check Center within the Department of 
28 Health and Human Services by Maine Emergency Medical Services within the Department 
29 of Public Safety to conduct background checks for persons seeking to be licensed or 
30 relicensed as an emergency medical services person or an emergency medical services 
31 ambulance operator.
