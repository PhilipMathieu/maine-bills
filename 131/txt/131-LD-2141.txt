17Sec. 3.  Commissioners to study watercraft inspection protocols and surface 
18use restrictions on inland waters of the State with invasive aquatic plants; 
19review of protocols and restrictions. The Commissioner of Environmental Protection 
20 and the Commissioner of Inland Fisheries and Wildlife shall review watercraft inspection 
21 protocols and surface use restrictions on inland waters of the State known to have invasive 
22 aquatic plants and make recommendations related to how these protocols and restrictions 
23 could be strengthened. The commissioners shall also evaluate how information about 
24 restrictions is communicated to members of the public. By January 1, 2025, the 
25 commissioners shall submit a report, including any suggested legislation, to the joint 
26 standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over inland fisheries and wildlife 
27 matters on their findings and recommendations with respect to watercraft inspection 
28 protocols and surface use restrictions on inland waters of the State with invasive aquatic 
29 plants and how information on restrictions is communicated to members of the public. The 
30 committee may report out legislation based on the report to the 132nd Legislature in 2025.

2 This bill appropriates $2,000,000 to the Invasive Aquatic Plant and Nuisance Species 
3 Fund to address the most serious aquatic plant infestations in inland waters of the State.  It 
4 requires the Department of Environmental Protection and the Department of Inland 
5 Fisheries and Wildlife to review and recommend more stringent watercraft inspection 
6 protocols on infested inland waters of the State and to review surface use restrictions and 
7 notification of those restrictions to members of the public.1
