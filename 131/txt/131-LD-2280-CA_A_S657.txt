22 SUMMARY
23 This amendment removes from the bill the provisions providing for the registration of
24 preapprenticeship training programs. It retains the current law allowing for certification of
25 these programs, It specifies that at least 51% of the funds provided for grants to support
26 preapprenticeship training programs must support training programs that have consistently
27 placed individuals in registered apprenticeship programs that lead to jobs that pay no less
28 than $3 5 per hour.

Fiscal Detail and Notes
This bill includes Other Special Revenue Funds allocations totaling $12,000,000 in fiscal year 2024-25 to the Maine
Apprenticeship Program within the Department of Labor to support preapprenticeship training programs. Of this
amount, $11,887,635 is allocated on a one-time basis for grants to support the training programs and $112,365 is
allocated on an ongoing basis for one Program Manager Employment & Training position and related All Other costs
to administer the grant program. A one-time transfer of $12,000,000 from the unappropriated surplus of the General
Fund to the Maine Apprenticeship Program is provided in fiscal year 2024-25 to fund the program.
