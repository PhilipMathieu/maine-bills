15 §6109-B. Contracts for large-scale extraction and transportation of water
16 Except as provided in subsection. 5, this section governs any contract or agreement
17 between a consumer owned water utility and another entity that involves the large-scale
18 extraction of water and the large-scale transportation of water.
19 1. Definitions. As used in this section, unless the context otherwise mdicates, the
20 following terms have the following meanings.
21 A. "Large-scale extraction of water" means the extraction of water that is required to
22 be permitted, certified, reported or approved pursuant to:
23 (1) The in-stream flow provisions of Title 38, section 470-H;
24 (2) The requirements for significant groundwater wells under Title 38, section
25 480-B;
26 (3) The site location of development law requirements for a development of state
27 or regional significance that may substantially affect the environment as provided
28 in Title 3 8, section 482, subsection 2; or
29 (4) The water withdrawal reporting program under Title 38, chapter 3, subchapter
30 1, article 4-B,
31 B. "Large-scale transportation of water" means the transportation of water for
32 commercial purposes by pipeline or other conduit or by tank vehicle or in a container,
33 greater in size than 10 gallons, beyond the boundaries of the municipality or township
34 in which the water is naturally located or of any bordermg municipality or township.

3 SUMMARY
4 This amendment replaces the bill, It amends the laws concerning contracts for large-
5 scale extraction and transportation of water to prohibit a water utility from entering into a
6 contract or agreement for the large-scale extraction of water and the large-scale
7 transportation of water for a term of more than 10 years and makes the prohibition
8 applicable to existing customers of the water utility. The amendment also broadens the
9 scope of this provision to apply to all water utilities rather than specifically to consumer-
10 owned water utilities. The amendment clarifies certain headnotes for provisions that apply
11 to only consumer-owned water utilities.
