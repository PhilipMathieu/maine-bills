1Emergency preamble.  Whereas, acts and resolves of the Legislature do not 
2 become effective until 90 days after adjournment unless enacted as emergencies; and
3Whereas, the Legislature enacted legislation in 2023 to extend the repeal date of the 
4 provisions of law establishing airboat noise limits but this legislation did not take effect 
5 before the provisions were repealed; and
6Whereas, the stakeholder group on airboat noise issues convened pursuant to Public 
7 Law 2021, chapter 166 and extended by Public Law 2021, chapter 585 and Public Law 
8 2023, chapter 209 has recommended that the decibel level limits for airboats be 
9 reestablished immediately; and
10Whereas, in the judgment of the Legislature, these facts create an emergency within 
11 the meaning of the Constitution of Maine and require the following legislation as 
12 immediately necessary for the preservation of the public peace, health and safety; now, 
13 therefore,
14Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
15Sec. 1.  12 MRSA §13068-A, sub-§10, ¶A, as amended by PL 2021, c. 166, §1, is 
16 further amended by amending the first blocked paragraph to read:
17 As used in this paragraph, "motorboat" does not include an "airboat, " which has the 
18 same meaning as  defined in paragraph A‑2 subsection 10-A.
19Sec. 2.  12 MRSA §13068-A, sub-§10-A is enacted to read:
2010-A.  Operating airboat that exceeds noise limits.  The following provisions govern 
21 airboat noise limits.
22 A.  A person may not operate an airboat:
23 (1)  If the noise level of the airboat exceeds 90 decibels as measured in a stationary 
24 sound level test as prescribed by SAE standards J-2005;
25 (2)  Between the hours of 7 p.m. and 7 a.m. in such a manner as to exceed a noise 
26 level of 75 decibels on the "A" scale as measured by the SAE standards J-1970, 
27 referred to in this paragraph as "the shoreline test," except to the extent necessary 
28 to achieve headway speed when leaving a boat launch or to move the airboat from 
29 a stationary position on a tidal flat; or
30 (3)  After 7 a.m. and before 7 p.m. in such a manner as to exceed a noise level of 
31 90 decibels as measured by the shoreline test, except to the extent necessary to 
32 achieve headway speed when leaving a boat launch or to move the airboat from a 
33 stationary position on a tidal flat. 
34 B.  This subsection does not apply to the operation of an airboat by a marine patrol 
35 officer appointed under section 6025, subsection 1, a game warden or a municipal law 
36 enforcement officer. 
37 C.  The following penalties apply to violations of this subsection. 
38 (1)  A person who violates this subsection commits a civil violation for which a 
39 fine of not less than $300 nor more than $500 may be adjudged.

8Emergency clause.  In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this legislation 
9 takes effect when approved.
10SUMMARY
11 This bill is reported out by the Joint Standing Committee on Inland Fisheries and 
12 Wildlife pursuant to Public Law 2021, chapter 585.  The bill provides that a person may 
13 not operate an airboat:
14 1.  If the noise level of the airboat exceeds 90 decibels;
15 2.  Between 7 p.m. and 7 a.m. if the noise level of the airboat exceeds 75 decibels, 
16 except to the extent necessary to achieve headway speed when leaving a boat launch or to 
17 move the airboat from a stationary position on a tidal flat; or
18 3.  Between 7 a.m. and 7 p.m. if the noise level of the airboat exceeds 90 decibels, 
19 except to the extent necessary to achieve headway speed when leaving a boat launch or to 
20 move the airboat from a stationary position on a tidal flat.
21 The bill also defines "airboat" and outlines penalties for violating these provisions and 
22 provides exceptions for certain law enforcement officers.
