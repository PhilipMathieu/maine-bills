31Sec. 5.  25 MRSA §2804-M is enacted to read:
32§2804-M.  Probation and parole officer basic training and certification
331.  Training and certification required.   Beginning January 1, 2025, as a condition 
34 to the continued employment of a person hired as a probation and parole officer, that person 
35 shall successfully complete, within the first 12 months of employment, a basic training 
36 course approved by the board and meet the certification standards established by the board 
37 pursuant to section 2803-A, subsection 5-C. Thereafter, as a condition to continued 
38 employment as a probation and parole officer, the officer shall satisfactorily maintain the 
39 basic certification. The board, under extenuating and emergency circumstances in 
40 individual cases, may extend the 12-month period for not more than 180 days. The board, 
41 in individual cases, may waive basic training requirements when the facts indicate that an 
42 equivalent course has been successfully completed in another state or federal jurisdiction.
