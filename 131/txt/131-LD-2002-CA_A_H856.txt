18 SUMMARY
19 This amendment, which is the majority report of the committee, establishes a grant
20 program within the Department of Education to provide grants to school administrative
21 units that contract for behavioral and mental health services provided by licensed
22 behavioral and mental health service providers. The amendment limits the use of funds
23 provided through the grant program to behavioral and mental health services that are not
24 otherwise covered or reunbursable through the MaineCare program and provides that funds
25 must be used in a manner that does not supplant qualified and available school
26 administrative unit behavioral and mental health staff.
27 The amendment provides that the amounts of the grants must be based on a school
28 administrative unit's population and provides ongoing funding for the grants starting in the
29 2nd year of the biennium.

Fiscal Detail and Notes
This bill includes ongoing General Fund appropriations of $1,353,000 per year beginning in fiscal year 2024-25 to the
School and Student Supports program within the Department of Education for grants to school administrative units for
contracted behavioral and mental health services for students. The grants are based on the population of each school
administrative unit.
