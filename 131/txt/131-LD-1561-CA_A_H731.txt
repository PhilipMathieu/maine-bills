6 SUMMARY
7 This amendment; which is the majority report of the committee, changes the bill to a
8 resolve and changes the title. It requires the Commissioner of Public Safety to convene a
9 stakeholder group to develop a process allowing persons prohibited from owning,
10 possessing or having under their control a firearm under Maine law the ability to petition
11 an appropriate government body or agency to have their full civil rights related to firearms
12 restored, The stakeholder group consists, at a minimum, of the Attorney General or the
13 Attorney General's designee, the Chief Justice of the Supreme Judicial Court or the chief
14 justice's designee, a representative of the Maine Coalition to End Domestic Violence, an
15 expert in the field of mental and behavioral health and a representative of the Maine
16 Prosecutors Association. The commissioner is required to submit by January 30, 2025 a
17 report describing the process developed by the stakeholder group, describing any potential
18 barriers to implementation and including any recommended legislation to the joint standing
19 committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over crimmal justice and public safety
20 matters. The committee may report out legislation related to the report to the 132nd
21 Legislature in 2025,
