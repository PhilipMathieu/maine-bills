1Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
2Sec. 1.  9-A MRSA §5-116-A, sub-§1, as enacted by PL 2009, c. 526, §1, is 
3 repealed and the following enacted in its place:
41.  Definitions.  For the purposes of this section, the following terms have the following 
5 meanings.
6 A. "Deferred interest provision" means a contractual provision that allows for interest 
7 to be charged on portions of the original balance on a medical credit card that have 
8 already been paid off.
9 B. "Health care provider" means a physician, health care practitioner, hospital, clinic, 
10 clinical laboratory, health care facility or other person or facility that provides health 
11 care services and is licensed or registered by the State.
12 C. "Medical credit card" means a credit card that is extended by a creditor to a 
13 consumer to defer payment for health care services.
14Sec. 2.  9-A MRSA §5-116-A, sub-§5 is enacted to read:
155.  Medical credit cards; requirements.  The following requirements apply to medical 
16 credit cards.
17 A. A health care provider may not offer or arrange for a medical credit card for a 
18 consumer, or establish or otherwise complete any portion of an application for a 
19 medical credit card on a consumer's behalf, in that health care provider's office, 
20 treatment area or other health care setting.  
21 B. A health care provider may not offer, arrange for or establish a medical credit card 
22 for a consumer that contains a deferred interest provision in the medical credit card 
23 contract. 
24 C. A health care provider may not charge the costs for health care services on a medical 
25 credit card at any time prior to the date upon which health care services are rendered 
26 or costs are incurred. 
27 D. Before accepting any payment from a consumer using a medical credit card, a health 
28 care provider shall screen the consumer for eligibility for charity care in accordance 
29 with any charity care policy adopted by the health care provider in accordance with the 
30 requirements of Title 22, sections 1715 and 1716.
31 E. A creditor that extends a medical credit card to a consumer and is attempting to 
32 collect unpaid payments in connection with the medical credit card from a consumer is 
33 subject to the provisions of Title 32, chapter 109-A.
34SUMMARY
35 This bill establishes the following requirements related to credit cards designed 
36 specifically for the payment of health care services.
37 1.  It prohibits a health care provider from offering or arranging for a medical credit 
38 card for a consumer, or establishing or otherwise completing any portion of an application 
39 for a medical credit card on a consumer's behalf, in that health care provider's office, 
40 treatment area or other health care setting.
