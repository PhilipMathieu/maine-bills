18 Whereas, the State's system of care that serves individuals who need residential
19 supports or long-term care is failing to meet the needs of the State's population, resulting
20 in hospitalized patients using inpatient beds when they need lower levels of care; and
21 Whereas, nursing facilities, assisted living facilities and other licensed residential
22 options that are intended to meet the needs of individuals who can no longer live safely at
23 home are unable to provide adequate access as a result of the closure of facilities and
24 reduced bed capacity due to financial and labor market pressures; and
25 Whereas, the State's laws governing residential supports have not been reviewed in
26 over a decade; and
27 Whereas, immediate review of the laws governing residential supports is necessary
28 in order to prevent more facility closures and bed capacity reduction; and
29 Whereas, in the judgment of the Legislature, these facts create an emergency within
30 the meaning of the Constitution of Maine and require the following legislation as
31 immediately necessaiy for the preservation of the public peace, health and safety; now,
32 therefore, be it

2 recommendations to the Legislature on changes to the State's laws in order to reflect the
3 State's changing demographics and labor market conditions and to ensure that the State has
4 the capacity to meet the current and future needs of individuals needing residential
5 supports. The commission shall review:
6 1. The demographics of the State and projected need for different levels of residential
7 supports, including but not limited to bed capacity at each level of support within a hospital
8 service area over the next 10 years;
9 2. The needs of the State's population needing residential supports and the barriers to
10 accessing appropriate levels of care;
11 3. Labor market conditions for residential supports, including projections over the next
12 10 years;
13 4. Laws governing clinical assessments and admission requirements for different levels
14 of residential supports;
15 5. Laws governing MaineCare coverage and payment;
16 6, Laws governing the growth of long-term care facilities, including but not limited to
17 certificate of need requirements and so-called budget neutrality;
18 7, Laws governing home care and hospice services to ensure that the State is
19 maximizing the use of those services; and
20 8. The role of technology to support the continuum of long-term care options for
21 elderly individuals and recommendations for the reimbursement of providers for expenses
22 for technology and innovation.

Legislative Cost/Study
Legislative Cost/Study
The general operating expenses of this study are projected to be $700 in fiscal year 2023-24 and $950 in fiscal year
2024-25. The Legislature's budget for the 2024-2025 biennium includes $14,500 in each of fiscal years 2023-24 and
2024-25 for the costs of legislative studies, as well as $38,909 of balances carried over from prior years for this
purpose, Whether these amounts are sufficient to fund all studies will depend on the number of studies authorized by
the Legislative Council and the Legislature. The additional costs of providing staffing assistance to the study during
the interim can be absorbed utilizing existing budgeted staff resources.
