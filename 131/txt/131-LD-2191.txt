1Emergency preamble.  Whereas, acts and resolves of the Legislature do not 
2 become effective until 90 days after adjournment unless enacted as emergencies; and
3Whereas, the State has recently experienced severe weather-related events that have 
4 caused power outages, flooding and other negative effects; and
5Whereas, recent severe weather-related events have significantly affected small 
6 business owners during the busiest revenue season for small businesses in the State; and
7Whereas, the loss of revenue experienced by small businesses as a result of recent 
8 severe weather-related events constitutes an emergency; and
9Whereas, in the judgment of the Legislature, these facts create an emergency within 
10 the meaning of the Constitution of Maine and require the following legislation as 
11 immediately necessary for the preservation of the public peace, health and safety; now, 
12 therefore,
13Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
14Sec. 1.  Small Business Weather Emergency Relief Program.  
151.  Program established.  The Small Business Weather Emergency Relief Program is 
16 established in the Department of Economic and Community Development as a nonlapsing 
17 General Fund program account administered by the department to provide relief payments 
18 to small businesses in the State affected by severe weather-related events and consists of 
19 money appropriated to it by the Legislature from the General Fund.
202.  Rules.  The Department of Economic and Community Development shall adopt 
21 rules necessary to carry out this section, including eligibility requirements, application 
22 processes and methods for determining award amounts, no later than March 30, 2024. Rules 
23 adopted pursuant to this subsection are routine technical rules as defined in the Maine 
24 Revised Statutes, Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 2-A.
25Sec. 2.  Appropriations and allocations.  The following appropriations and 
26 allocations are made.
