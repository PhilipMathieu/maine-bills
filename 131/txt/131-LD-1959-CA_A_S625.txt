21 SUMMARY
22 This amendment replaces the bill and changes the title. The amendment allows a
23 political party to determine whether that party will use a primary election or a nomination
24 caucus to determine candidates for federal, state or county office, including presidential
25 candidates. The party must determine at the state convention held between March 1st and
26 August 1st biennially during each general election year whether that party will hold a
27 primary election or nomination caucus for the general election following the next general
28 election after the party's convention. The party also is required to establish the date for
29 such a nomination caucus and must incorporate the party's fmal determination into the party
30 rules. Candidates for a nomination caucus are required to qualify in the same way as
31 candidates for a primary election.
