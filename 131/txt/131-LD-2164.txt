3CHAPTER 39
4MAINE-IRELAND TRADE COMMISSION
5§1031. Commission established
6 The Maine-Ireland Trade Commission, referred to in this chapter as “the commission,” 
7 is established by Title 5, section 12004-K, subsection 10-A to promote cooperation 
8 between this State and the country of Ireland. 
9§1032.  Membership; terms
101.  Membership.  The commission consists of 9 members appointed as follows:
11 A.  Two members of the Senate, appointed by the President of the Senate, including 
12 one member of the party holding the largest number of seats in the Legislature and one 
13 member of the party holding the 2nd largest number of seats in the Legislature;
14 B.  Two members of the House of Representatives, appointed by the Speaker of the 
15 House of Representatives, including one member of the party holding the largest 
16 number of seats in the Legislature and one member of the party holding the 2nd largest 
17 number of seats in the Legislature; and
18 C.  The following 5 members, appointed by the Governor:
19 (1)  A representative of a public institution of higher education;
20 (2)  A representative of a statewide chamber of commerce;
21 (3)  Two members representing Irish-American communities, organizations or 
22 interests; and
23 (4)  One member of the public.
24 In making the appointments under this subsection, the appointing authorities shall, to the 
25 greatest extent possible, consider and appoint individuals with knowledge of or current or 
26 past involvement in organizations that promote Irish or Irish-American affairs.  
272.  Terms.  Legislative members of the commission serve during the term of office for 
28 which they were elected.  The members of the commission appointed pursuant to 
29 subsection 1, paragraph C serve 4-year terms. 
30§1033. Vacancies
31 A member appointed to fill a vacancy occurring prior to the expiration of the term for 
32 which the member’s predecessor was appointed serves only for the remainder of that term. 
33 The vacancy must be filled in the same manner as the original appointment was made.  
34§1034. Chair
35 The commission shall elect a chair from among its members.  The chair may appoint 
36 from among the commission members subcommittees and subcommittee chairs at the 
37 chair's discretion.

1§1035. Quorum
2 A majority of the members of the commission constitutes a quorum and a quorum is 
3 required for the transaction of the business of the commission. 
4§1036. Duties
5 The commission shall work to advance bilateral trade and investment between Maine 
6 and Ireland; initiate joint action of policy issues of mutual interest to Maine and Ireland; 
7 promote business and academic exchanges between Maine and Ireland; encourage mutual 
8 economic support between Maine and Ireland; encourage mutual investment in the 
9 infrastructure of Maine and Ireland; and address other issues as determined by the 
10 commission. 
11§1037. Report
12 The commission shall submit a report to the Governor and the joint standing committee 
13 of the Legislature having jurisdiction over innovation, development, economic 
14 advancement and business matters by February 1, 2025 and annually thereafter regarding 
15 the activities of the commission during the preceding calendar year.  The report must 
16 include recommendations as determined appropriate by the commission to effectuate its 
17 purpose. 
18§1038. Funds
19 The commission may raise funds, through direct solicitation or other fundraising 
20 methods, alone or with other groups, and accept gifts, grants and bequests from individuals, 
21 corporations, foundations, governmental agencies and public and private organizations and 
22 institutions. The commission may expend those funds for purposes consistent with this 
23 chapter.
