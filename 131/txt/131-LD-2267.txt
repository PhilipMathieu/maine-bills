19SUMMARY
20 This bill implements certain recommendations of the Commission Regarding Foreign-
21 trained Physicians Living in Maine and is being reported out by the Joint Standing 
22 Committee on Health Coverage, Insurance and Financial Services pursuant to Resolve 
23 2023, chapter 93, section 8. The committee is reporting out the bill for the sole purpose of 
24 having a bill printed that can be referred to the committee for an appropriate public hearing 
25 and subsequent processing in the normal course and has not taken a substantive position on 
26 the bill.  The committee is taking this action to ensure clarity and transparency in the 
27 legislative review of the proposals contained in the bill.
28 The bill does the following. 
29 1. It directs the Department of Labor to contract with a 3rd-party entity to provide an 
30 assistance program and a clinical readiness program for international medical graduates. 
31 2. It directs the Finance Authority of Maine to provide a loan program on a pilot basis 
32 for the purpose of providing financial assistance to attend an approved education program 
33 in this State in order to join the State's health care workforce, to international medical 
34 graduates residing in this State who are unable to qualify for any existing federal or state 
35 education loan programs.
36 3. It directs the Office of Policy Innovation and the Future to coordinate with 
37 appropriate educational institutions to develop programs for international medical 
38 graduates residing in this State to gain entry into and complete educational programs in 
39 alternative health care careers, such as physician assistant, nurse or nurse practitioner 
40 programs, to ensure that the State is effectively incorporating immigrants into the State's 
41 health care workforce and communities and to strengthen the State's economy.1
