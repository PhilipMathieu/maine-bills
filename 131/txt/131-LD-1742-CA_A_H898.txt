15 SUMMARY
16 This amendment replaces the bill and does the following,
17 1, It adds "private safety agencies" and "public safety answering points" to the
18 definition of "providers," requiring the agencies and answering points to offer critical
19 incident stress management services and peer team support services,
20 2, It allows providers to partner with an organization or other providers to provide the
21 required critical incident stress management services and peer team support services.
22 3, It charges the Commissioner of Public Safety instead of the Director of the Mame
23 Emergency Management Agency within the Department of Defense, Veterans and
24 Emergency Management with overseeing the implementation of the provisions in the
25 amendment, It also specifies that the commissioner may hire at least one person to take on
26 these duties,
27 4. It requires the Commissioner of Public Safety in implementing and overseeing the
28 requirements in the amendment to consult with the Director of the Maine Emergency
29 Management Agency, a critical incident stress management peer support person, an expert
30 in the field of mental and behavioral health and an expert in the coordination of events
31 following public disasters,
32 5. It adds peer team support services to the list of services covered under applicable
33 confidentiality rules.
34 6,It changes the standards established by rule by the Commissioner of Public Safety
3 5 for training critical incident stress management peer support persons from routine technical
36 to major substantive rules.
37 7. It makes other technical changes,
