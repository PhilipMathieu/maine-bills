1Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
2Sec. 1.  22 MRSA §2496, sub-§1, as enacted by PL 2005, c. 140, §1, is amended to 
3 read:
41.  Department rulemaking.  The department is authorized and empowered to  may 
5 make and enforce all necessary rules and regulations for the administration of this chapter, 
6 and may rescind or modify such rules and regulations from time to time as may be in the 
7 public interest, insofar as such action is not in conflict with any of the provisions of this 
8 chapter.  Rules adopted pursuant to this subsection are routine technical rules as defined in 
9 Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 2‑A, except as otherwise provided in subsection 1-A.
10Sec. 2.  22 MRSA §2496, sub-§1-A is enacted to read:
111-A.  Youth camps; rulemaking.  The department may make and enforce all necessary 
12 rules for the regulation of youth camps in the State under this chapter.  Rules adopted 
13 pursuant to this subsection are major substantive rules as defined in Title 5, chapter 375, 
14 subchapter 2-A.
15SUMMARY
16 This bill changes the rules adopted by the Department of Health and Human Services 
17 for the regulation of youth camps from routine technical rules to major substantive rules 
18 and makes other technical changes to conform to the drafting standards for the Maine 
19 Revised Statutes.
