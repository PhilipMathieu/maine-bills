8 SUMMARY
9 This amendment, which is the majority report of the committee, replaces the bill, The
10 amendment updates the term "Maine Native American studies" with "Wabanaki studies"
11 in the laws regarding elementary and secondary education requirements and provides that,
12 in addition to being a. required component in Maine studies, Wabanald studies may be
13 taught as a component in other content areas, as appropriate. The amendment also directs
14 the Commissioner of Education to consider the inclusion ofWabanaki studies in content
15 areas other than social studies in the system of learning results when conducting the
