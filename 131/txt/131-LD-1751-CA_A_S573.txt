21 SUMMARY
22 This amendment replaces the bill with a resolve that requires the Department of Health
23 and Human Services to conduct an analysis of the feasibility of implementing an ambulance
24 service assessment fee to maximize federal Medicaid matching funds to be distributed to
25 ambulance service providers. The department must also explore the potential for voluntary
26 programs to increase funding to governmental ambulance service providers by
27 implementing an intergovernmental transfer program. The department must submit a
28 report with results and recommendations from the analysis to the joint standing committee
29 of the Legislature having jurisdiction over health and human services matters no later than
30 January 10,2025.

Fiscal Detail and Notes
This bill includes a one-time General Fund appropriation to the Department of Health and Human Services of
$162,500 in fiscal year 2024-25 to contract with an entity to conduct an analysis of the feasibility of implementing an
ambulance assessment fee that generates federal Medicaid matching funds to be distributed to ambulance service
providers and to explore the potential for voluntary programs to increase funding to governmental ambulance service
providers using intergovernmental transfer programs. Federal Expenditures Fund allocations are also included for the
FMAP match.
