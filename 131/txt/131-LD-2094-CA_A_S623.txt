19 SUMMARY
20 This amendment, which is the majority report of the committee, makes the following
21 changes to the resolve.
22 1. It removes from the study: an analysis of how to develop and support law
23 enforcement entities in rural communities; an analysis of the current educational, social
24 service, public health, criminal justice, drug enforcement and economic needs of
25 communities and law enforcement entities and opportunities to address those needs; an
26 analysis of media coverage in the State and the impact of media on public perception of
27 law enforcement officers; an analysis of current community policing, social services and
28 crime prevention programs throughout the State and recommendations for future programs;
29 and an analysis of how funding could be provided for these removed study initiatives.
30 2. It removes the Governor's appointment authority for members of the stakeholder
31 group.
32 3. It removes the following members from the stakeholder group: an individual
33 representing the Maine Law Enforcement Coalition; an individual representing a statewide
34 mental health organization; an individual representing a statewide association that
35 represents retired law enforcement officers; and an individual representing a statewide
36 private law enforcement organization.
37 4. It adds the following members to the stakeholder group: an individual representing
38 a coalition of mental health providers in the State who specialize in treating first responders
39 as their sole practice; an individual representing a postsecondary educational institution in
40 the State with programs focused on law enforcement or mental health; a representative
41 from the M&me Chiefs of Police Association; a representative from the Maine Sheriffs'
42 Association; a student representing a secondary school program focused on law
