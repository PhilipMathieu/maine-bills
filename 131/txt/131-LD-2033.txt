1Preamble.  The Constitution of Maine, Article IX, Section 23 requires that real estate 
2 held by the State for conservation or recreation purposes may not be reduced or its uses 
3 substantially altered except on the vote of 2/3 of all members elected to each House.
4Whereas, certain real estate authorized for conveyance by this resolve is under the 
5 designations described in the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 12, section 598-A; and
6Whereas, the Director of the Bureau of Parks and Lands within the Department of 
7 Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry may sell or exchange lands with the approval of 
8 the Legislature in accordance with the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 12, section 1851; now, 
9 therefore, be it
10Sec. 1. Director of Bureau of Parks and Lands authorized to convey certain 
11land in Woodstock.  Resolved:  That the Director of the Bureau of Parks and Lands 
12 within the Department of Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry may by quitclaim deed 
13 convey without covenant, on other terms and conditions as the director may direct, the 
14 State's interest in a 0.34-acre parcel on the Dolloff Road in Woodstock near Concord Pond 
15 identified as Parcel 2 of Parcel A on Sheet #5 of a survey recorded as Plan #1401 in the 
16 Oxford County Registry of Deeds.  The cost of a survey of the property boundaries and 
17 recording costs may not be paid by the bureau.
18SUMMARY
19 This resolve authorizes the Director of the Bureau of Parks and Lands within the 
20 Department of Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry to convey the State's interest in a 
21 0.34-acre parcel of land in Woodstock.
