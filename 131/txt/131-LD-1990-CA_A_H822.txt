Sec, 5. Duties. Resolved: That, in conducting the holistic evaluation of the social 
work licensure system required by this resolve, the commission shall review and evaluate: 
I. The purposes and goals of licensure in the State, including: 
A. The historic development of the concept and practice of licensure in the State; and 
B. The systems and institutions that have historically defined the purposes and goals 
and the concept of licensure and have administered and mediated the design and 
execution of the licensure process; 
2, The steps taken by individuals in order to obtain and retain licensure, including but 
not limited to: 
A. All legal requirements of the process of licensure prior to and upon the effective 
date of this resolve, including but not limited to educational requirements, examination 
requirements, supervision requirements and other requirements set forth in law or rule; 
and 
B. Any steps that, while not technically required, are taken by those pursuing a license 
prior to and during the process of licensure with the intent of aiding the potential 
licensees with the licensure process; 
3. The efficacy of the steps under subsection 2 in meeting the purposes and goals of 
the licensure process under subsection I, and any barriers to these steps, including but not 
limited to cost; 
4. The efficacy of the steps under subsection 2 in creating a social work workforce that 
meets the needs of the State, including but not limited to the need for: 
A. Workforce equity, including reviewing and evaluating the quantity, type and 
distribution of social workers patticularly with respect to equity factors such as race, 
ethnicity, socioeconomic status, gender, sexuality 01· sexual orientation, disability, 
primary language and geographic location; and 
B. Social work services, pa1ticulal'ly with respect to the equity factors in paragraph A, 
and the systems that currently provide those services; and 
5. Alternative pathways to Ii censure that may be required following the removal of any 
patticular step of the current licensure process, including but not limited to examination 
requirements. 
The commission. shall also review and identify best practices for licensure of social 
workers adopted in other states and the District of Columbia, including statutory or 
regulatoty requirements or other policies or processes for licensnre, and shall develop 
recommendations for suggested legislation or other changes to the process for licensure of 
social workers in the State.
