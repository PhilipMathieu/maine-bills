Sec. 14. Transition provisions. The following provisions govern the transition of 
the Maine Commission on Indigent Legal Services to the Maine Commission on Public 
Defense Services. 
I. The Maine Commission on Public Defense Services is the successor in every way 
to the powers, duties and functions of the former Maine Commission on Indigent Legal 
Services. 
2. Each member of the Maine Commission on Indigent Legal Services appointed by 
the Governor and confirmed by the Legislature pursuant to the Maine Revised Statutes, 
Title 4, section 1803 is declared to be a member of the Maine Commission on Public 
Defense Services for the remainder of that member's unexpired term. 
3. All existing mies, regulations and procedures in effect, in operation or adopted in or 
by the Maine Commission on Indigent Legal Services or any of its administrative units or 
officers are declared in effect and continue in effect until rescinded, revised or amended by 
the proper authority. 
4. All existing contracts, agreements and compacts currently in effect for the Maine 
Commission on Indigent Legal Services continue in effect. 
5. Any positions authorized and allocated subject to the personnel laws to the former 
Maine Commission on Indigent Legal Services are transferred to the Maine Commission 
on Public Defense Se!'Vices and continue to be authorized. 
6. All records, property and equipment previously belonging to or allocated for the use 
of the former Maine Commission on Indigent Legal Services become, on the effective date 
of this Act, pa1t of the property of the Maine Commission on Public Defense Services. 
7. All existing forms, licenses, letterheads and similar items bearing the name of or 
refe11'ing to the "Maine Commission on Indigent Legal Services" may be used by the Maine 
Commission on Public Defense Services until existing supplies of those items are 
exhausted.

SUMMARY 
This amendment, which is the unanimous rep01t of the committee, replaces the bill and 
changes the title. The amendment changes the name of the Maine Commission on Indigent 
Legal Services to the Maine Commission on Public Defense Services in order to more 
cleal'iy describe the commission's mission and functions to the public. 
The amendment funds the establishment of a new public defender office to serve 
Aroostook County; a new public defender office to serve Penobscot and Piscataquis 
counties; a new Public Service Coordinator I position to perform investigative se1vices for 
the existing public defender office in Kennebec County; and new positions in the Maine 
Commission on Indigent Legal Setvices' central office designed to assist with the fo1mation 
and implementation of new public defender offices and training of new public defenders 
and reduces funding in the All Other line categ01y to pay the costs associated with these 
initiatives. The amendment also directs the State Controller to cany forward any remaining 
balance in the Personal Services line categ01y in the Maine Commission on Indigent Legal 
Services program to fund costs associated with establishing public defender offices. 
The amendment also adds an emergency preamble and emergency clause.
