20 SUMMARY
21 This amendment, which is the majority report of the committee, replaces the bill and
22 changes the title. The amendment makes the following changes to the bill.
23 1. It changes all references to the Maine Commission on Indigent Legal Services in the
24 bill to the Maine Commission on Public Defense Services to reflect the enactment of Public
25 Law 2023, chapter 558.
26 2. It clarifies that the commission's purpose is to provide high-quality, effective and
27 efficient representation and to promote due process for persons who receive indigent legal
28 services in parity with the resources of the State and consistent with federal and state
29 constitutional and statutory obligations.
30 3. It amends the definition of "employed counsel" to mean an attorney employed by
31 the commission to provide legal services directly to persons who are eligible to receive
32 indigent legal services in civil proceedings and defines "public defender" to mean an
33 attorney employed by the commission to provide legal services directly to persons who are
34 eligible to receive indigent legal services in criminal and juvenile proceedings, An attorney
35 employed by the commission may serve in both capacities.
36 4. It removes the provision of the bill that proposed to make the 2 members of the
37 commission who are attorneys that provide indigent legal services voting members of the
38 commission.
39 5. It clarifies that, to ensure an adequate pool of qualified attorneys, the commission
40 has a duty to develop training and evaluation programs for attorneys throughout the State
41 to provide representation in criminal, juvenile, child protective, involuntary commitment
