4 (6)  The value of the property is more than $500 and the person has 2 or more prior 
5 convictions for any combination of the Maine offenses listed in this subparagraph 
6 or for engaging in substantially similar conduct to that of the Maine offenses listed 
7 in this subparagraph in another jurisdiction.  The Maine offenses are:  theft; any 
8 violation of section 401 in which the crime intended to be committed inside the 
9 structure is theft; any violation of section 405 in which the crime intended to be 
10 committed inside the motor vehicle is theft; any violation of section 651; any 
11 violation of section 702, 703 or 708; or attempts to commit any of these crimes.  
12 Section 9‑A governs the use of prior convictions when determining a sentence.  
13 Violation of this subparagraph is a Class C crime; or
14SUMMARY
15 This bill stipulates that a person with 2 or more prior convictions may not be charged 
16 with a Class C crime if the person commits theft of property with a value of $500 or less.
