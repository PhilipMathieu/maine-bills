10 SUMMARY
11 This amendment replaces the bill and changes the title. It provides that, beginning
12 January 1, 2025, a salaried employee who works in an executive, administrative or
13 professional capacity must earn at least $55,068 annually or the annualized rate under the
14 federal Fair Labor Standards Act, whichever is higher, to be exempt from the laws
15 governing the minimum wage and overtime pay. The amendment provides for an annual
16 mcrease, begimiing January 1, 2026, based on the annual percentage increase, if any, in
17 certain weekly earnings as published by the United States Department of Labor, Bureau of
18 Labor Statistics.

Fiscal Detail and Notes
This bill includes ongoing General Fund appropriations of $1,414,501 in fiscal year 2024-25 to the Executive Branch
Departments and Independent Agencies - Statewide program within the Department of Administrative and Financial
Services (DAFS) for the costs associated with increasing the annual rate that an employee who works in an executive,
administrative or professional capacity must earn in order to be exempt from the laws governing the minimum wage
and overtime pay from 3,000 times the State's minimum hourly wage ($42,450 in 2024) to $55,068 effective January
1, 2025. The estimated cost provided by DAFS assumes 207 positions would become eligible for overtime pay under
this law, that each employee would work one hour of overtime per workday at an overtime rate of $39.71 per hour,
The estimated cost provided by DAFS also includes $691,809 per year as a result of the increase in retirement costs.
