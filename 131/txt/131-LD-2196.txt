Page 1 - 131LR3006(01)1Emergency preamble.  Whereas, acts and resolves of the Legislature do not 
2 become effective until 90 days after adjournment unless enacted as emergencies; and
3Whereas, the Constitution of Maine, Article X, Section 6 requires the Chief Justice 
4 of the Supreme Judicial Court to arrange the Constitution of Maine and submit the 
5 arrangement to the Legislature once every 10 years, unless sooner authorized by the 
6 Legislature; and
7Whereas, the Chief Justice performed this requirement in the spring of 2023; and
8Whereas, in November 2023, 2 amendments to the Constitution of Maine were 
9 approved by the voters at referendum; and 
10Whereas, in order to ensure that the Constitution of Maine is as up to date as possible 
11 and avoid printing of versions of the Constitution of Maine that are incomplete and 
12 incorrect, it is necessary that the Chief Justice prepare an arrangement of the Constitution 
13 of Maine that includes the 2 amendments that were approved in November 2023; and
14Whereas, in the judgment of the Legislature, these facts create an emergency within 
15 the meaning of the Constitution of Maine and require the following legislation as 
16 immediately necessary for the preservation of the public peace, health and safety; now, 
17 therefore, be it
18Sec. 1.  Chief Justice authorized to prepare arrangement of Constitution of 
19Maine reflecting changes approved at November 2023 referendum.  Resolved:  
20 That, pursuant to the Constitution of Maine, Article X, Section 6, the Chief Justice of the 
21 Supreme Judicial Court is authorized to arrange the Constitution of Maine and include in 
22 that arrangement the amendments to the Constitution of Maine approved by the voters at 
23 referendum in November 2023.
24Sec. 2.  Amendments to be included in arrangement of Constitution of 
25Maine.  Resolved:  That the following constitutional resolutions approved by the voters 
26 at the November 2023 referendum must be included in the arrangement authorized pursuant 
27 to section 1:
28 1. Constitutional Resolution 2023, chapter 1: RESOLUTION, Proposing an 
29 Amendment to the Constitution of Maine to Require All Provisions in the Constitution to 
30 Be Included in the Official Printing; and
31 2. Constitutional Resolution 2023, chapter 2: RESOLUTION, Proposing an 
32 Amendment to the Constitution of Maine Regarding the Timing of Judicial Review of the 
33 Determination of the Validity of Written Petitions.
34Sec. 3.  Submission to Legislature. Resolved:  That the Chief Justice of the 
35 Supreme Judicial Court shall submit the arrangement authorized by this resolve to the 
36 Legislature no later than 30 days following the effective date of this resolve.
37Emergency clause.  In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this legislation 
38 takes effect when approved.
