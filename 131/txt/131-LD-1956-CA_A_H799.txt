SUMMARY 
This amendment makes the following changes to the bill. 
1. It adds a definition of "in person" as it relates to a visit between an optometrist and 
a patient. 
2. It provides that an optometrist-patient relationship begins when there has been an in­
person encounter between the optometrist and the patient, unless the standard of care 
requires that a patient be seen without an in-person encounter, such as in an emergent 
situation. 
3, It changes the definition of "therapeutic pharmaceutical II to include a pharmaceutical 
agent used to diagnose ocular conditions or diseases. 
4. It establishes that minimum standards for an eye examination include a physical 
examination of each eye in an in-person clinical setting and, if a refraction is pe1formed, 
the perfo1mance of an objective and subjective refraction when practicable. 
5. It provides that the State Board of Optometry may investigate complaints against 
any person relating to actions or activities involving a kiosk or telehealth and makes other 
changes to the authority of the board to resolve complaints and impose discipline. 
6. It clarifies the timing of when an optometrist who is closing a practice is required to 
create and maintain a.written directive identifying the optometrist who is willing to assume 
responsibility for the practice, to maintain continuity of treatment and to transfer medical 
info1mation. 
7. It makes technical changes to cross-references in the bill and removes u1111ecessary 
language.
