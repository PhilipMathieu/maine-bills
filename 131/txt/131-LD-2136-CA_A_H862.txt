15 SUMMARY
16 This amendment increases the ongoing appropriation to support emergency shelter
17 operations in the bill from $10,000,000 to $12,500,000 and requires the Maine State
18 Housing Authority to reserve $2,500,000 annually exclusively for distribution to low-
19 barrier emergency shelters. The amendment requires the Statewide Homeless Council to
20 convene a stakeholder group to develop a 10-year plan to address the root causes of
21 homelessness. The stakeholder group is required to notify the Joint Select Committee on
22 Housing of all meetings of the stakeholder group. The stakeholder group is required to
23 hold at least 4 meetings prior to the council's submitting a report to the Joint Select
24 Committee on Housing by November 6, 2024, The amendment also requires the Maine
25 State Housing Authority to amend its rules to ensure that $2,500,000, or an equivalent
26 percentage of the funds appropriated for the shelter operation subsidy, is reserved
27 exclusively for distribution to low-barrier shelters,

Fiscal Detail and Notes
This bill includes ongoing General Fund appropriations totaling $12,500,000 per year beginning in fiscal year 2024-25
to the Shelter Operating Subsidy program within the Maine State Housing Authority (MSHA) to support emergency
shelter operations. Of this amount, $10,000,000 is appropriated on an ongoing basis for the operation of homeless
shelters and $2,500,000 is appropriated on an ongoing basis for low-ban-ier shelters.
Additional costs to MSHA associated with the rulemaking process and with supporting the Statewide Homeless
Council in its work to develop a 10-year plan to address the root causes of homelessness in Maine can be absorbed
within existing budgeted resources,
