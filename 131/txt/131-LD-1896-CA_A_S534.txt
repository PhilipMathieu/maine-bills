11 Amend the bill by striking out the title and substituting the following:
12 'Resolve, Directing the Workers' Compensation Board to Analyze Data on the
13 Adequacy of Certain Maine Workers' Compensation Benefits'
14 Amend the bill by striking out everything after the title and inserting the following:
15 'See. 1. Workers' Compensation Board to identify and conduct an analysis
16 of claims and payments. Resolved: That the Workers' Compensation Board, referred
17 to in this resolve as "the board," using data supplied by insurers, 3rd-party administrators,
18 group self-insurers and individual self-insured employers, shall identify and analyze the
19 compensation and benefits provided to an individual claimant pursuant to the Maine
20 Revised Statutes, Title 39-A, section 212 for total incapacity, section 213 for partial
21 incapacity and section 215 for death and any other relevant data and available reports. For
22 each claimant, the analysis must include, but is not limited to, data and reports relating to
23 the following components:
24 1. The claim identification number assigned by the board;
25 2. The claim identification number assigned by the insurer, 3rd-party administrator,
26 group self-insurer or individual self-insured employer;
27 3. The date of injury;
28 4. The average weekly wage;
29 5. The compensation rate;
30 6. For a claimant pursuant to Title 39-A, section 212, the number of weeks of
31 compensation and benefits paid;
32 7. For a claimant pursuant to Title 39-A, section 213, the number of weeks of
33 compensation and benefits paid and the number of weeks for which the benefit was 100%
34 partial;

12 Sec. 2. Updates; report; legislation. Resolved: That the board, within existing
13 resources, shall provide monthly updates to the joint standing committee of the Legislature
14 having jurisdiction over workers' compensation matters on the identification of data and
15 reports and the analysis conducted under section 1. No later than August 16, 2025, the
16 board shall submit a final report to the committee with its findings, recommendations and
17 suggested legislation. The report must include:
18 1. A thorough analysis of the data and reports that were considered, identification of
19 data or other areas that require further study and recommendations on any changes or
20 adjustments to workers' compensation benefits in order to ensure claimants are receiving
21 adequate benefits;
22 2. A thorough analysis of whether the M.aine Workers' Compensation Act of 1992
23 provides substantial protection for workers who have suffered work-related injuries and
24 diseases at an affordable cost to employers, including whether the workers' compensation
25 system can provide the income support that injured workers require as a result of their
26 injuries at a cost no greater than the median cost in other states; and
27 3. Information regarding the retroactive application of workers' compensation
28 legislation based on the analysis under this resolve and an evaluation of the costs of
29 potential retroactive application.
30 The committee may report out legislation to the 132nd Legislature in 2026 related to
31 the subject matter of the report.'
