11 SUMMARY
12 This amendment makes the following changes to the requirements in the bill,
13 1. It makes the Public Utilities Commission primarily responsible for coordination of
14 the E-9-1-1 and 9-8-8 systems,
15 2. It changes the requirement for the Department of Health and Human Services to
16 establish 6 crisis receiving centers to a requirement to establish 2 crisis receiving centers,
17 one m Aroostook County and one in Penobscot County,
18 3. It adds the Department of Defense, Veterans and Emergency Management, Maine
19 Bureau of Veterans' Services and the violence prevention program within the Department
20 of Health and Human Services to the list of entities with which the Office of Violence
21 Prevention may collaborate,
22 4, It strikes language authorizmgthe Office of Violence Prevention to collaborate with
23 individuals, educational institutions, health care providers and organizations with expertise
24 in violence prevention and gun safety,
25 5, It requires, by February 1, 2027, the Department of Health and Human Services to
26 submit to the joint standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over health
27 and human services matters a report that includes information regarding the number of
28 grants awarded under the grant program to organizations to conduct violence intervention
29 initiatives and the amount of funding provided to each grantee; a description of violence
30 intervention initiatives funded, including the number of individuals served and the
31 communities served by each funded initiative; and recommendations regarding the
32 continuation of the grant program and recommendations for any changes to the program,
33 6. It repeals the grant program within the Office of Violence Prevention on June 30,
34 2027.
35 7. It strikes language providing that the Office of Violence Prevention may receive
3 6 funding from any source and requiring the office to identify and apply for available federal
37 grants and other funding to further its work to prevent violence.
38 8. It clarifies that the funding to reduce waiting lists for and expand access to
39 medication management sei-vices provided by the Department of Health and Human
40 Services, Office of Behavioral Health is ongoing funding,

Fiscal Detail and Notes
Part A of this bill includes ongoing General Fund appropriations to the Department of Health and Human Services
(DHHS) of $17,342,127 in fiscal year 2024-25 to strengthen mental health crisis intervention mobile response
services with mobile outreach peer workers, certified intentional peer support specialists, recovery coaches, mental
health police liaisons co-located in law enforcement agencies, community debriefing, critical incident response and
for ancillary services. This funding is intended to provide services 24 hours a day, 7 days a week. The bill also
includes a one-time allocation of $100,000 to the Public Utilities Commission (PUC) in fiscal 2024-25 for contracted
consulting services to coordinate the 911 and 988 systems and to prepare reporting on the status of the coordination
efforts. The PUC has indicated that it has sufficient reserves in its unobligated funds to fund the allocation,
Part B of the bill includes ongoing General Fund appropriations to the DHHS of $3,848,490 in fiscal year 2024-25 to
establish 2 crisis receiving centers, one in Aroostook County and.one in Penobscot County, Part B also includes a one-
time General Fund appropriation of $1,900,000 in fiscal year 2024-25 for the additional start-up costs for the 2 new
crisis receiving centers, • " -•
Part C of the bill includes ongoing General Fund appropriations to the DHHS of $7,215,453 in fiscal year 2024-25 to
create a new Office of Violence Prevention within the DHHS. The part also includes a one-time General Fund
appropriation of $1,000,000 in fiscal year 2024-25 for the required technology changes.
