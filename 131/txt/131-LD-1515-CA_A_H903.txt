19 SUMMARY
20 This amendment, which is the majority report of the committee, replaces the bill and
21 changes the title, It adds an emergency preamble and emergency clause and does the
22 following.
23 1. It establishes a permanent Maine Emergency Medical Services Commission to
24 monitor and evaluate the State's emergency medical services system on. a contmuing basis
25 and to provide recommendations to the appropriate state agencies and to the Legislature
26 regarding necessary changes in the emergency medical services system.
27 2, It permits the municipal officers of a municipality to adopt and post a plan
28 stipulatmg the method by which transporting emergency medical services will be delivered
29 within the municipality. A municipality is not required to directly or indirectly provide or
30 fund the delivery of transporting emergency medical services within the municipality.
31 3, It amends the Maine Emergency Medical Services Act of 1982 to authorize an
32 emergency medical services provider to render emergency medical services within a
33 hospital or health care facility where the provider is a contractor of the hospital or facility,
34 4, It directs the Department of Public Safety, Maine Emergency Medical Sendces to
35 conduct a funding needs analysis of communities seeking to engage in regional
36 collaboration or the adoption of a regional model in the delivery of emergency medical
37 services and to submit a report describing the findings of its analysis and any associated
38 recommendations on or before March 1, 2025 to the joint standmg committee of the
39 Legislature having jurisdiction over public safety matters. The committee may report out
40 related legislation.

Legislative Cost/Study
The bill provides an ongoing appropriation of $2,100 in 2023-24 and $3,540 in 2024-25 for the costs of Legislators
serving on the Maine Emergency Medical Services Commission.
Fiscal Detail and Notes
The bill includes a one-time General Fund appropriation of $150,000 in fiscal year 2024-25 to the Department of
Public Safety for a consultant to perform a funding needs analysis of communities seeking to engage in regional
collaboration or the adoption of a regional model in the delivery of emergency medical services,
Additional costs to the Office of the Governor, the Maine Community College System, the Department of Health and
Human Services and the Department of Public Safety associated with a representative serving on the commission can
be absorbed within existing budgeted resources.
