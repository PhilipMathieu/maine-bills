1Emergency preamble.  Whereas, acts and resolves of the Legislature do not 
2 become effective until 90 days after adjournment unless enacted as emergencies; and
3Whereas, there is a shortage of electricians in the State; and
4Whereas, this legislation will enable applicants for a journeyman-in-training 
5 electrician license to use completion of approved career and technical education programs 
6 toward qualification for the license examination and to receive a significant credit of work 
7 hours toward the work hours required for licensure; and
8Whereas, it is important that this legislation take effect immediately in order to allow 
9 more applicants for journeyman-in-training electrician licensure to qualify sooner; and
10Whereas, in the judgment of the Legislature, these facts create an emergency within 
11 the meaning of the Constitution of Maine and require the following legislation as 
12 immediately necessary for the preservation of the public peace, health and safety; now, 
13 therefore,
14Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
15Sec. 1.  32 MRSA §1202-B, sub-§3, ¶B, as enacted by PL 2023, c. 327, §2, is 
16 amended by amending subparagraph (3) to read:
17 (3)  Completion of at least 2,000 work hours in the field of electrical installations 
18 as a licensed helper electrician and a vocational-electrical program of a state 
19 department of corrections; or
20Sec. 2.  32 MRSA §1202-B, sub-§3, ¶B, as enacted by PL 2023, c. 327, §2, is 
21 amended by amending subparagraph (4) to read:
22 (4)  Comparable work experience, education or training, or any combination of 
23 comparable work experience, education or training, completed within the State or 
24 outside the State, that is acceptable to the board. ; or
25Sec. 3.  32 MRSA §1202-B, sub-§3, ¶B, as enacted by PL 2023, c. 327, §2, is 
26 amended by enacting a new subparagraph (5) to read:
27 (5)  Completion of a secondary school career and technical education electrical 
28 program approved pursuant to Title 20‑A, section 8306‑B and completion of at 
29 least 2,000 work hours in the field of electrical installations as a licensed helper 
30 electrician.  An individual applying pursuant to this subparagraph may take the 
31 examination upon graduating from the program and is credited 1,000 work hours 
32 in the field of electrical installations.  The individual must complete any remaining 
33 work hours prior to applying for a journeyman-in-training license.
34Emergency clause.  In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this legislation 
35 takes effect when approved.
