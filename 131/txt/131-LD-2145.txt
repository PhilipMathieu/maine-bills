7Sec. 3.  Resolve 2023, c. 126, §4, amended. Resolved:  That Resolve 2023, c.

9Sec. 4. Appointments; chair; convening of commission; advisory council. 
10Resolved: That all appointments must be made no later than 30 days following the 
11 effective date of this resolve. The appointing authorities shall notify the Secretary of State 
12 once all appointments have been completed. When appointment of all members is 
13 complete, the Secretary of State shall call and convene the first meeting of the commission 
14 no later than December 1, 2023. The Secretary of State shall name 5 members to constitute 
15 an executive committee, which shall act as chair of the commission. The Secretary of State, 
16 with advice from the commission, also may appoint honorary members and shall establish 
17 an advisory council composed of people who are especially interested in the celebration of 
18 the United States Semiquincentennial consisting of nonvoting members of the public to 
19 seek input on decisions made or considered by the commission.
20Sec. 4.  Resolve 2023, c. 126, §8, amended.  Resolved:  That Resolve 2023, c. 
21 126, §8 is amended to read:
22Sec. 8.  Expenses; compensation. Resolved: That the executive committee 
23 established under section 4 shall draft a commission budget and may pay necessary 
24 expenses of the commission from the fund established under section 12. The Secretary of 
25 State may request additional appropriations from the Legislature for necessary expenses of 
26 the commission.
27 Members of the commission are eligible to receive a legislative per diem as defined in 
28 the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 5, section 12002, subsection 3. Pursuant to Title 5, section 
29 12002-B, legislative per diem may only be paid for a member’s actual attendance at a 
30 meeting of the commission, a public hearing held by the commission, an advisory board 
31 meeting held by the commission or participation in official business of the commission 
32 required by law or by rule of the commission that is necessary to fulfill the statutory 
33 responsibilities of the commission. 
34 Members may only be compensated up to a maximum of 4 meetings per month.  This 
35 section may not be interpreted to be inconsistent with the conflict of interest provisions in 
36 The Maine Revised Statutes, Title 5, section 18-A.
37Sec. 5.  Resolve 2023, c. 126, §9, repealed.  Resolved:  That Resolve 2023, c. 
38 126, §9 is repealed.
