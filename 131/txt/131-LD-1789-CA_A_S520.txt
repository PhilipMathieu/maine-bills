14 a resolve that requires the Department of Administrative and Financial Services, office of
15 cannabis policy to convene a stakeholder group to examine marijuana-related and caimabis-

17 Revised Statutes to determine whether there are marijuana-related or cannabis-related
18 crimes currently in effect that should be amended or removed. The stakeholder group must
19 also examine whether there are any existing activities related to marijuana or cannabis that
20 should be made criminal. The stakeholder group must include, at a minimum,
21 representatives from the Maine Prosecutors Association and the Department of Public
22 Safety, Maine Dmg Enforcement Agency. The office of cannabis policy is required to

Fiscal Detail and Notes
The Office ofCannabis Policy (OCP) within the Department of Administrative and Financial Services has indicated
that current staff does not have the necessary criminal law expertise to conduct a review of the relevant sections of the
Maine Criminal Code and the Maine Revised Statutes within the timeline required by the bill. The bill includes a one-
time General Fund appropriation of $200,000 to OCP in fiscal year 2024-25 for contracted legal services.
Any additional costs to the Department of Public Safety associatied with serving on a stakeholder group can be
absorbed within existing budgeted resources.
