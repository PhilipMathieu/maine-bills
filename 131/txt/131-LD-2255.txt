1Emergency preamble.  Whereas, acts and resolves of the Legislature do not 
2 become effective until 90 days after adjournment unless enacted as emergencies; and
3Whereas, this resolve reestablishes the Task Force on Accessibility to Appropriate 
4 Communication Methods for Deaf and Hard-of-hearing Patients to study accessibility to 
5 appropriate communication methods for deaf and hard-of-hearing patients in health care 
6 settings and how that accessibility may be improved; and
7Whereas, the study must be initiated before the expiration of the 90-day period in 
8 order to provide sufficient time for the study to be completed and a report submitted in time 
9 for submission to the next legislative session; and
10Whereas, in the judgment of the Legislature, these facts create an emergency within 
11 the meaning of the Constitution of Maine and require the following legislation as 
12 immediately necessary for the preservation of the public peace, health and safety; now, 
13 therefore, be it
14Sec. 1. Task force established.  Resolved:  That the Task Force on Accessibility 
15 to Appropriate Communication Methods for Deaf and Hard-of-hearing Patients, referred 
16 to in this resolve as "the task force," is established.
17Sec. 2. Task force membership.  Resolved: That, notwithstanding Joint Rule 353, 
18 the task force consists of 10 members appointed as follows:
19 1.  One member of the Senate who serves on the Joint Standing Committee on Health 
20 and Human Services, appointed by the President of the Senate;
21 2.  One member of the House of Representatives who serves on the Joint Standing 
22 Committee on Health and Human Services, appointed by the Speaker of the House;
23 3.  One member of the public who is a person who is deaf or hard of hearing, appointed 
24 by the Speaker of the House;
25 4.  One member representing the Department of Health and Human Services, office of 
26 aging and disability services, appointed by the Commissioner of Health and Human 
27 Services;
28 5.  One member representing Disability Rights Maine, appointed by the Commissioner 
29 of Health and Human Services;
30 6.  One member representing the Maine Educational Center for the Deaf and Hard of 
31 Hearing and the Governor Baxter School for the Deaf, appointed by the Commissioner of 
32 Health and Human Services;
33 7.  One member who is a practicing American Sign Language interpreter who has 
34 national certification as a core certification health care interpreter from the Certification 
35 Commission for Healthcare Interpreters, appointed by the Commissioner of Health and 
36 Human Services;
37 8.  One member representing a hospital system with a language access plan that is 
38 considered an appropriate model for hospitals, appointed by the Commissioner of Health 
39 and Human Services;
40 9.  One member representing the University of Maine System with national 
41 certification as a core certification health care interpreter from the Certification 
Page 2 - 131LR3094(01)
42 Commission for Healthcare Interpreters and experience in American Sign Language 
43 medical interpreter training, appointed by the Commissioner of Health and Human 
44 Services; and
4 10.  One member representing the Department of Labor who works with compliance 
5 issues regarding deaf and hard-of-hearing persons, appointed by the Commissioner of 
6 Labor.
7Sec. 3. Chairs.  Resolved: That the Senate member is the Senate chair and the House 
8 of Representatives member is the House chair of the task force.
9Sec. 4. Appointments; convening of task force.  Resolved: That all 
10 appointments must be made no later than 30 days following the effective date of this 
11 resolve.  The appointing authorities shall notify the Executive Director of the Legislative 
12 Council once all appointments have been completed.  After appointment of all members, 
13 the chairs shall call and convene the first meeting of the task force.  If 30 days or more after 
14 the effective date of this resolve a majority of but not all appointments have been made, the 
15 chairs may request authority and the Legislative Council may grant authority for the task 
16 force to meet and conduct its business.
17Sec. 5. Duties.  Resolved: That the task force shall study accessibility to appropriate 
18 communication methods for deaf and hard-of-hearing patients in health care settings and 
19 how that accessibility may be improved.  The task force shall consider, but is not limited 
20 to considering, the following:
21 1.  The availability of American Sign Language interpreters in health care settings;
22 2.  The availability of other communication technologies in health care settings, such 
23 as video interpreters, automatically generated voice transcriptions and automatically 
24 generated captions;
25 3.  Staff education and training programs on overcoming barriers to health care 
26 experienced by deaf and hard-of-hearing patients; and
27 4.  Successful models for overcoming barriers to health care experienced by deaf and 
28 hard-of-hearing patients.
29Sec. 6. Staff assistance.  Resolved: That the Legislative Council shall provide 
30 necessary staffing services to the task force, except that Legislative Council staff support 
31 is not authorized when the Legislature is in regular or special session.
32Sec. 7. Report.  Resolved: That, no later than November 6, 2024, the task force shall 
33 submit a report that includes its findings and recommendations, including suggested 
34 legislation, for presentation to the joint standing committee of the Legislature having 
35 jurisdiction over health and human services matters.  The joint standing committee of the 
36 Legislature having jurisdiction over health and human services matters is authorized to 
37 report out legislation related to the report to the 132nd Legislature in 2025.
38Emergency clause.  In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this legislation 
39 takes effect when approved.

1SUMMARY
2 This resolve is reported out by the Joint Standing Committee on Health and Human 
3 Services pursuant to Resolve 2023, chapter 97. The resolve reestablishes the Task Force 
4 on Accessibility to Appropriate Communication Methods for Deaf and Hard-of-hearing 
5 Patients to study accessibility to appropriate communication methods for deaf and hard-of-
6 hearing patients in health care settings and how that accessibility may be improved. The 
7 task force is required to submit a report and suggested legislation by November 6, 2024 to 
8 the joint standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over health and human 
9 services matters, which is authorized to report out legislation based on the report to the 
10 132nd Legislature in 2025.
