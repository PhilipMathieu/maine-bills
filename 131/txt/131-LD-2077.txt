2Sec. 1.  35-A MRSA §301, sub-§7 is enacted to read:
37.  Prohibition on cost recovery for new gas service mains and gas service lines.  
4 Beginning February 1, 2025, any charge for costs associated with new gas service mains

9 customer's meter and "gas service main" means a large pipeline in which gas is carried for 
10 distribution through gas service lines.

10Sec. 7. Public Utilities Commission inquiry; costs associated with gas 
11system expansion. The Public Utilities Commission shall conduct an inquiry focused on 
12 customer costs associated with gas system expansion and implications for gas use in the 
13 State. In conducting the inquiry, the commission shall:
141. Request for information. Issue a request for information to appropriate 
15 stakeholders and consider relevant information and documents submitted in response to the 
16 request for information;
172. Projected impacts. Consider the projected impacts on gas consumption in the State 
18 over the next 10, 20 and 30 years from factors related to climate change, including but not 
19 limited to the mandatory greenhouse gas emissions reduction levels in the Maine Revised 
20 Statutes, Title 38, section 576-A, and policies, including energy efficiency, beneficial 
21 electrification and weatherization policies. As part of its consideration under this 
22 subsection, the commission shall review:
23 A. How the State's gas utilities are considering and preparing for potential reductions 
24 in the number of gas utility customers and declines in overall usage attributable to 
25 factors related to climate change and how the gas utilities should be preparing;
26 B. The projected impacts of declining gas consumption levels on distribution service 
27 rates paid by gas utility customers, especially low-income customers and customers 
28 with high energy burdens; 
29 C. The projected impacts of increasing distribution service rates on gas utilities' 
30 customer base, especially low-income customers and customers with high energy 
31 burdens; and
32 D. The projected demographic composition of gas utilities' diminishing customer base 
33 over the next 10, 20 and 30 years;
343. Potential rate and regulatory reforms. Consider potential rate and regulatory 
35 reforms to protect gas utility customers from risk associated with continued gas system 
36 expansion, particularly stranded gas system investments, including but not limited to:
37 A. Changes to depreciation schedules to better reflect the projected useful life of gas 
38 investments; and
39 B. Criteria by which the commission ought to balance the risk of stranded asset costs 
40 for gas investments between gas utility shareholders and gas utility customers; and
414. Draft report. Publish a draft report related to the inquiry and accept and consider 
42 public comments.

36SUMMARY
37 This bill provides that, beginning February 1, 2025, any charge for costs associated 
38 with new gas service mains and gas service lines for residential and commercial gas service 
39 is unreasonable for inclusion in rates and prohibited.  It provides that, beginning January 1, 
40 2025, the Public Utilities Commission may not approve a gas utility to furnish service or 
41 serve customers in a municipality that is outside of the utility's service area on June 30, 
42 2024.  Beginning January 1, 2025, it prohibits a gas utility from offering or providing a 
43 promotional allowance to customers or potential customers.37
