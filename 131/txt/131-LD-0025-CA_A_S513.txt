24 SUMMARY
25 This amendment, which replaces the bill, makes a technical change to the bill and
26 clarifies that a member of a federally recognized Indian nation, tribe or band in Maine is
27 not required to pay a fee for admission to not only state-owned parks and historic sites, but
28 also camping areas and beaches managed by the State. The amendment also specifies that
29 to qualify for free admission, a person is requu-ed to present a qualifying tribal
30 identification, as approved by the respective tribal government, to the park attendant or
31 other designee of the Director of the Bureau of Parks and Lands within the Department of
32 Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry.

Fiscal Detail and Notes
This bill provides free admission for members of federally recognized Indian nations, tribes and bands to state-owned
parks, historic sites, camping areas and beaches. This is estimated to reduce General Fund revenue by $22,131 in
fiscal year 2024-25 and $29,508 starting in fiscal year 2025-26. This estimate assumes that tribal members will use
state-owned parks, historic sites, camping areas and beaches at the same rate as Maine residents and nonresidents.
