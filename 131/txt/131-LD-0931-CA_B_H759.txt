1 SUMMARY
2 This amendment, which is a minority report of the committee, replaces the bill, which
3 is a concept draft, with a resolve. It requires the Department of Corrections to conduct a
4 review of its reentry planning and support services to identity gaps that may exist between
5 the services that are provided and the needs of residents of correctional facilities who are
6 transitioning from incarceration to employment. The department must work with key
7 stakeholders, including, but not limited to, community-based organizations that provide
8 services to and advocate for persons who are or have been incarcerated and employers and
9 organizations representing employers that are interested in hiring persons who are or have
10 been incarcerated. The department must submit a report of the department's findings, an
11 evaluation of any gaps that may exist between the services the department provides and the
12 needs of persons transitioning from incarceration to employment and the department's
13 recommendations for how best to meet those needs, by January 31, 2025, to the joint
14 standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over criminal justice matters, The
15 committee may submit legislation related to the report to the 132nd Legislature in 2025.
