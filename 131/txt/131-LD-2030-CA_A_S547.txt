15 Amend the bill by inserting after the title and before the enacting clause the following:
16 'Emergency preamble. Whereas, acts and resolves of the Legislature do not
17 become effective until 90 days after adjournment unless enacted as emergencies; and
18 Whereas, in recent months, the State has experienced multiple significant storm
19 events causing widespread and devastating flooding and damagmg public and private
20 infrastructure across the State with particular impact along the coast, which has experienced
21 historically high and dangerous tides; and
22 Whereas, with an ever-increasing frequency of such storm events and the associated
23 risks to persons, property and resources, the State, local governments and citizens of the
24 State must respond quickly and effectively during these storms and be able to enhance the
25 resilience of public and private infrastructure to the effects of these storms; and
26 Whereas, proposed changes to the Natural Resources Protection Act, which will serve
27 to better prepare the State and mfrastructure across the State to withstand such storm events,
28 must take effect immediately to facilitate the development of critical coastal and inland
29 resiliency measures; and
30 Whereas, in the judgment of the Legislature, these facts create an emergency within
31 the meaning of the Constitution of Maine and require the following legislation as
32 immediately necessary for the preservation of the public peace, health and safety; now,
33 therefore,'

35 This amendment changes the title of the bill, adds an emergency preamble and
36 emergency clause and amends the bill as follows.
37 1. It clarifies the provision in the bill exempting from permit requirements under the
3 8 Natural Resources Protection Act emergency activities to alleviate flood events to require
39 a local government conducting or overseemg such activity to notify and maintain
40 communication with the Department of Environmental Protection.

16 replaced where the height of the pier, wharf or dock is increased by no more than the
17 amount necessary for the bottom of the lowest horizontal structural component of the deck
18 to be 4 feet above base flood elevation, subject to additional specified limitations.

Fiscal Detail and Notes
This bill will exempt certain activities from requiring a Department of Environmental Protection permit. Reducing
number of permits issued will result in a minor loss of Other Special Revenue Funds revenue from these permits and
minor savings in staff time from fewer permit reviews.
