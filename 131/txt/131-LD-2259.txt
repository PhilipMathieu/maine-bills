3ยง12.  Compensation for services related to veterans' benefits matters
41.  Definitions.  As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 
5 following terms have the following meanings.
6 A.  "Compensation" means payment of money, a thing of value or a financial benefit.
7 B.  "Person" means an individual, corporation, business trust, estate, trust, partnership, 
8 limited liability company, association, joint venture, public corporation, government 
9 or governmental subdivision, agency or instrumentality or any other legal or 
10 commercial entity.
11 C.  "Veterans' benefits matter" means the preparation, presentation or prosecution of a 
12 claim affecting an individual who has filed or expressed an intent to file a claim for a 
13 benefit, program, service, commodity, function or status, entitlement to which is 
14 determined under the laws and regulations administered by the United States 
15 Department of Veterans Affairs or the United States Department of Defense pertaining 
16 to veterans, their dependents, their survivors and any other individual eligible for such 
17 benefits.
182.  Prohibitions.  A person may not:
19 A.  Receive compensation for preparation, presentation or prosecution of, or advising, 
20 consulting or assisting an individual with, a veterans' benefits matter, except as 
21 permitted under federal law;
22 B.  Receive compensation for referring an individual to another person to prepare, 
23 present or prosecute, or advise, consult or assist the individual with, a veterans' benefits 
24 matter;
25 C.  Receive, with respect to an individual's veterans' benefits matter, compensation for 
26 services rendered before the date on which a notice of disagreement, decision review 
27 or appeal is filed, whichever occurs first;
28 D.  Guarantee, either directly or by implication, that any individual is certain to receive 
29 specific veterans' benefits or that any individual is certain to receive a specific level, 
30 percentage or amount of veterans' benefits; and
31 E.  Receive excessive or unreasonable fees as compensation for preparation, 
32 presentation or prosecution of, or advising, consulting or assisting an individual with, 
33 a veterans' benefits matter. The factors articulated in 38 Code of Federal Regulations, 
34 Section 14.636 (2024) govern determinations of whether a fee is excessive or 
35 unreasonable.
363.  Memorialization of terms.  A person seeking to receive compensation for 
37 preparation, presentation or prosecution of, or advising, consulting or assisting an 
38 individual with, a veterans' benefits matter shall, before rendering any services, 
39 memorialize in a written agreement signed by both parties that adheres to all criteria 
40 specified in 38 Code of Federal Regulations, Section 14.636 (2024) all terms regarding the 
41 individual's payment of fees for services rendered.

14.  Penalty.  A violation of this section constitutes a violation of the Maine Unfair 
2 Trade Practices Act.
3SUMMARY
4 This bill provides that a person may not receive compensation for services related to 
5 veterans' benefits matters except as permitted under federal law.  It also provides that a 
6 person may not receive compensation for referring an individual to another person to 
7 provide services related to veterans' benefits matters.  It provides that a person seeking to 
8 receive compensation for services related to veterans' benefits matters must, before 
9 rendering any services, memorialize in a written agreement that is signed by both parties 
10 and that complies with federal regulations all terms regarding the payment of fees for 
11 services rendered.  It provides that a person may not receive excessive or unreasonable fees 
12 for services related to veterans' benefits matters.  A violation of the provisions of the bill 
13 constitutes a violation of the Maine Unfair Trade Practices Act.
